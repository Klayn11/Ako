Ferrari

Ferrari is an Italian company that specializes in the production of high-end sports cars and is also involved in motor racing, including Formula 1. The company was founded in 1939 by Enzo Ferrari, who began his career in motorsports as a racing driver, and then began to create cars in order to achieve success in racing.

Ferrari's main characteristics:

Ferrari, with its unique history and reputation, continues to be one of the symbols of the automobile industry, embodying excellence and the pursuit of leadership.

Cars: The company produces exclusive sports cars that are characterized by high performance, stylish design and advanced technology. Ferrari is known for its powerful supercars such as the Ferrari 488, Ferrari LaFerrari and Ferrari SF90 Stradale. Formula One: Ferrari is one of the most successful teams in the history of Formula One. The Scuderia Ferrari team is a strong competitor in the world championship, with a large number of wins and titles. Luxury and prestige: Ferrari cars are associated with luxury and status. Many models are produced in limited quantities, which makes them even more exclusive.
